ShelfSpace: a $497 AI-visibility audit for DTC brands, built end to end

Consumers increasingly ask ChatGPT and Claude what to buy — and most brands have no idea whether AI assistants recommend them, their competitors, or nobody. ShelfSpace turns that blind spot into a productized audit.

Role
Founder — everything

TL;DR

I identified an emerging category (GEO — Generative Engine Optimization), designed a $497 productized audit around it, built the AI measurement pipeline, wrote the direct-response sales page, and wired Stripe checkout. One person, offer to product to payment.

The business problem

AI assistants are becoming a shopping channel. When someone asks "what's the best air purifier under $200?", the answer is a shortlist — and brands not on it lose the sale before a website is ever visited. Unlike Google rankings, this "AI shelf space" is invisible to brands: there's no Search Console for ChatGPT.

SEO agencies were starting to talk about GEO, but almost nothing existed as a concrete, affordable deliverable for small DTC brands. That gap is the product.

Designing the offer

  • Productized, not consulting: a fixed-scope, fixed-price ($497) audit that can be sold from a landing page without sales calls — the right shape for a solo operator.
  • Deliverable-first: the buyer gets a concrete report — where their brand shows up across AI assistants, how they compare to named competitors, and a prioritized fix list.
  • Priced against the alternative: $497 is positioned against agency GEO retainers, making it an easy first purchase for a founder or marketing lead.

What I built

  • A two-pass measurement pipeline on the Claude API: pass one generates the buying-intent questions a brand's customers actually ask; pass two scores how visibly the brand (versus competitors) appears in AI answers to those questions.
  • A report generator that turns raw scores into a client-ready document with findings and recommendations — the deliverable that justifies the price.
  • A long-form sales page in classic direct-response structure: problem → mechanism → proof → deliverable → price → FAQ. Every section answers one specific objection.
  • Stripe checkout and a delivery workflow, so the business can take money and fulfill without me building anything else first.

Marketing strategy

ShelfSpace sits at the bottom of a funnel I also built: AI Rank, a free AI-visibility checker, generates awareness and demonstrates the problem in 30 seconds; ShelfSpace monetizes the brands who see a bad score and want the full picture. Free tool → paid audit is a deliberate product-led pipeline.

Results

  • Complete productized business shipped solo: offer, product, sales page, and payment flow.
  • Live and sellable at shelfspace-eta.vercel.app.
