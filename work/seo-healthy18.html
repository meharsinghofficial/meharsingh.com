Building organic search growth for a wellness brand

Health & wellness is one of the most competitive — and most scrutinized — spaces in organic search. At Healthy18 I owned the SEO program end to end: strategy, content, and the technical foundation.

Role
SEO & Content Marketing Specialist
Timeline
Jun 2021 – Sep 2022
Focus
Organic growth, content, technical SEO
Stack
SEMrush, Ahrefs, GSC, GA
TL;DR

Owned organic growth for a health & wellness brand for 15 months: keyword and competitor research, an SEO content engine targeting high-intent queries, technical SEO fixes, and reporting that tied content investment to traffic and rankings.

The challenge

Wellness SERPs are dominated by high-authority publishers, and Google's health-content standards (E-E-A-T) punish thin content. A smaller brand can't win head-on — it has to pick high-intent, winnable queries and support them with a technically clean site.

What I did

  • Keyword & competitor strategy: used SEMrush and Ahrefs to map the query landscape, find gaps competitors under-served, and prioritize by intent and winnability rather than raw volume.
  • Content engine: planned and published SEO-optimized blog content targeting high-intent health & wellness queries — briefs, on-page optimization, and internal linking built in from the start.
  • Technical SEO: implemented site-structure improvements, meta-tag cleanup, internal linking architecture, and page-speed optimization.
  • Measurement: tracked rankings, organic traffic, and content performance in Google Analytics and Search Console, reporting on keyword movement and content ROI to guide the next quarter's plan.

Results

  • Roughly doubled organic search sessions in 15 months through keyword strategy, on-page optimization, and technical fixes.
  • Improved keyword rankings across the target query set and surfaced high-intent keyword gaps that fed the SEM and growth strategy.
  • A repeatable brief → publish → measure content workflow.

What this shows

Full-stack SEO ownership — strategy, content, technical, and measurement — in a category where content quality standards are unforgiving. This foundation is also what led me to GEO/AI-search work like ShelfSpace: organic visibility is moving to a new surface, and the same discipline applies.
