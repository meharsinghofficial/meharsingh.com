Turning a manual lead process into a CRM-driven pipeline

Service businesses lose revenue in the gaps: leads that never get a follow-up, quotes that go quiet, renewals nobody tracks. For FirstClass Insurance Agency, the fix was structure — a CRM built around how the agency actually sells.

TL;DR

Designed and implemented a CRM system for an insurance agency: structured lead capture, defined pipeline stages, and follow-up workflows — replacing a manual, memory-dependent process with one the whole agency can see and run.

The problem

Insurance selling is follow-up-driven: quotes have shelf lives, renewals are predictable revenue, and referral leads go cold fast. Without a system, all of that lives in inboxes and individual memory — which means leakage that never shows up on any report, because nobody is measuring it.

What I built

  • Lead capture: a single intake path so every inquiry — phone, web, referral — lands in the CRM with a source attached, making marketing channels measurable.
  • Pipeline stages: stages matching the agency's real sales motion (new lead → contacted → quoted → negotiating → bound / lost), so the owner can see exactly where revenue is stuck.
  • Follow-up workflows: task and reminder automation for quote follow-ups and renewal dates — the two moments where insurance revenue is most often lost.
  • Visibility: pipeline reporting that turns "how's business?" from a feeling into numbers: leads by source, conversion by stage, and value in pipeline.
